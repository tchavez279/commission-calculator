Commission vs Salary: Which Sales Job Should You Take?

Choosing between a commission-heavy and salary-heavy role is one of the biggest career decisions in sales. Here's a practical framework to help you make the right choice for your situation.

📅 May 2025·⏱ 7 min read

Two job offers land in your inbox on the same day. One pays $80,000 base with a small commission kicker. The other pays $40,000 base with uncapped commission and a $120,000 OTE. Which do you take?

The honest answer is: it depends on factors most people don't think to ask about. This guide walks through the key considerations so you can make a decision based on your actual situation rather than just the headline numbers.

The Core Trade-Off

Every commission vs. salary decision comes down to one fundamental trade-off: security vs. upside. A higher base gives you predictable income regardless of how your month goes. A higher commission percentage gives you the ability to earn far more — but also far less — depending on your performance and circumstances.

Neither is inherently better. The right answer depends on your financial situation, your confidence in the product, your experience level, and your personal tolerance for income volatility.

When a Salary-Heavy Role Makes More Sense

  • You're new to sales or the industry. Building skills takes time. A higher base gives you runway to learn without financial pressure forcing you into shortcuts.
  • You have significant fixed expenses. Mortgage, kids, debt repayments — if missing a commission month would genuinely hurt you, prioritise the base.
  • The product is unproven. Selling something new and unvalidated is hard. Commission-heavy pay on a product that hasn't found market fit is a gamble.
  • The sales cycle is long. Enterprise deals that take 9–12 months to close make commission-only structures genuinely difficult to survive financially.
  • You value stability and predictability. There is no shame in valuing financial certainty — especially if you perform better without income anxiety.

When a Commission-Heavy Role Makes More Sense

  • You have a proven track record. If you've consistently hit or exceeded quota at previous roles, commission-heavy pay is where you'll out-earn your peers.
  • The product has strong market fit. A product that sells itself lowers the risk of commission-based income dramatically.
  • You have financial cushion. 3–6 months of savings removes the desperation that kills commission sales performance.
  • The commission is uncapped. Uncapped commission with a strong product is one of the best wealth-building vehicles in any profession.
  • You thrive under performance pressure. Some salespeople genuinely perform better when pay is directly tied to results.

A Framework for Comparing Offers

When you have two offers with different base/commission splits, don't just compare the OTE figures. Model multiple scenarios:

ScenarioHigh Base Offer ($80k base)High Commission Offer ($40k base, $120k OTE)
Miss quota badly (50%)~$90,000~$60,000
Hit 80% of quota~$96,000~$88,000
Hit 100% of quota~$100,000~$120,000
Hit 130% of quota~$106,000~$160,000+

Notice that the high-base offer wins at low attainment but the high-commission offer wins significantly at high attainment. The question is: which scenario is more likely for you, in this role, at this company?

The Questions That Actually Matter

Before accepting either offer, get answers to these:

  • What % of reps hit quota last year? What was the median attainment?
  • Is the commission uncapped? Are there accelerators above quota?
  • How long is the average sales cycle?
  • What is the average deal size and close rate?
  • How is quota set — historically, theoretically, or based on territory?
  • What happened to top performers last year — did they stay or leave?

The most revealing question: "Can I speak to a current rep about their experience with the comp plan?" A company that's hesitant to let you talk to existing salespeople is a red flag.

The Bottom Line

A commission-heavy role at a company with a proven product, achievable quotas, uncapped upside, and a strong team is one of the best financial opportunities in any career. The same structure at a company with an unproven product, inflated quotas, and high rep turnover is a trap.

The headline numbers — base, OTE, commission rate — are less important than the underlying conditions that determine whether those numbers are achievable. Do your research, ask the hard questions, and use our commission calculator to model realistic scenarios before you decide.
